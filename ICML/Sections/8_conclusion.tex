\section{Conclusion}
In this work, we present a fully open-source reinforcement learning recipe for training code search agents, and release the \methodname model family that demonstrates state-of-the-art localization performance without relying on complex scaffolds dependent on programming language-specific static analysis tools unlike prior work. By leveraging a simple agent scaffold equipped with \emph{just} a terminal tool, \methodname achieves remarkable localization efficacy. Our experiments on SWE-Bench Lite, Verified, and Pro show that our 1.7B, 4B and 14B models not only outperform significantly larger open-source models (both pre-trained and post-trained variants) across numerous different complex scaffolds, but also close the performance gap with some proprietary closed-source LLMs. Our analysis reveals the advantages of augmenting issue resolution agents with relevant localized context both in terms of efficiency and performance. Furthermore, we study the fine-grained changes in model behaviour during RL in terms of the command-line utilities leveraged for localization, and reveals the feasibility of developing even simpler scaffolds with strong performance on this task. We include additional discussion about limitations and directions for future work in Appendix~\ref{appendix:future_work}. Finally, we publicly release our code, training data, and model weights for future work to build upon, and leverage our infrastructure to train effective LLM agents for software engineering tasks.
