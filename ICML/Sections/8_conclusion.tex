\section{Conclusion}

In this work, we present a fully open-source reinforcement learning recipe for training code search agents, and release the \methodname model family that demonstrates state-of-the-art localization performance without relying on any complex, language-specific static analysis tools unlike prior work. By leveraging a simple agent scaffold equipped with just the terminal tool, \methodname achieves remarkable localization efficacy. Our experiments on SWE-Bench Lite, Verified, and Pro show that our 4B and 14B models not only outperform significantly larger open-source models (both pre-trained and post-trained variants) across numerous different complex scaffolds, and also closes the performance gap with proprietary closed-source LLMs. Our analysis reveals fine-grained behaviour changes in the command-line utilities leveraged by the model during the course of the training run. We will publicly release our code, training data, and model weights for future work to build upon and leverage our infrastructure to train effective LLM agents for software engineering tasks.
