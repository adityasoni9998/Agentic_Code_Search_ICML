\section{Conclusion}
We present \methodname: a fully open-source reinforcement learning recipe for training code search agents, and release the \methodname model family that demonstrates state-of-the-art localization performance without relying on complex scaffolds dependent on programming language-specific tools as done by prior work. By leveraging a simple agent scaffold equipped with \emph{just} a terminal tool, \methodname achieves remarkable performance. Our experiments on SWE-Bench Verified, Pro, and Lite show that models trained with our recipe not only outperform significantly larger open-source base and post-trained LLMs across various complex scaffolds, but also close the performance gap with some proprietary closed-source LLMs. Our analysis reveals the advantages of augmenting issue resolution agents with relevant localized context both in terms of efficiency and performance. We also study the fine-grained changes in model behaviour during RL in terms of the command-line utilities leveraged by the agent, revealing the feasibility of developing even simpler scaffolds. Appendix~\ref{appendix:future_work} includes additional discussion on limitations and future research directions. We publicly release our code, data, and model weights for future work to build upon.
